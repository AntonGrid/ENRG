# 14. Proof-of-Production

## 14.1 Overview

Proof-of-Production (PoP) is the fundamental protocol object of the ENRG Protocol.

A Proof-of-Production is a cryptographically signed statement confirming that a measurable physical event has occurred.

Proof-of-Production establishes the foundation for protocol trust, policy evaluation, and on-chain execution.

---

## 14.2 Objectives

Proof-of-Production SHALL provide:

- Authenticity
- Integrity
- Replay protection
- Device attribution
- Measurement verification
- Auditability

Every Proof SHALL be independently verifiable.

---

## 14.3 Proof Lifecycle

Every Proof SHALL follow the lifecycle below.

```
Generate
      │
      ▼
Sign
      │
      ▼
Transmit
      │
      ▼
Verify
      │
      ▼
Policy Evaluation
      │
      ▼
Execute
```

Each stage SHALL complete successfully before the next stage begins.

---

## 14.4 Mandatory Fields

Every Proof-of-Production SHALL contain at least:

- Device Identifier
- Protocol Version
- Timestamp
- Nonce
- Measurement
- Digital Signature

Future protocol versions MAY introduce additional fields while preserving compatibility.

---

## 14.5 Proof Generation

Proofs SHALL be generated by the originating device.

The device SHALL digitally sign every Proof using its private key.

Private keys SHALL never leave the device.

---

## 14.6 Proof Verification

Every received Proof SHALL be verified before policy evaluation.

Verification SHALL include:

- Digital Signature
- Timestamp
- Nonce
- Device Identity
- Manifest Compatibility

Proofs failing verification SHALL be rejected.

---

## 14.7 Replay Protection

Replay protection SHALL be provided through unique nonces and timestamp validation.

Previously accepted Proofs SHALL NOT be accepted again.

---

## 14.8 Policy Evaluation

Successful verification SHALL NOT automatically authorize protocol execution.

Every verified Proof SHALL be evaluated by the Policy Engine.

The Policy Engine SHALL determine whether the Proof satisfies protocol policy.

---

## 14.9 Smart Contract Execution

Only Proofs accepted by the Policy Engine MAY result in Smart Contract execution.

Proof generation SHALL NOT imply token minting.

Token issuance SHALL remain subject to protocol policy and on-chain validation.

---

## 14.10 Security Requirements

Every Proof SHALL satisfy the following security requirements.

- Authenticity
- Integrity
- Non-repudiation
- Replay protection
- Independent verification

Implementations SHALL reject Proofs violating any security requirement.

---

## 14.11 Audit Requirements

Every accepted Proof SHOULD remain auditable.

Audit information SHOULD include:

- Device Identifier
- Timestamp
- Proof Identifier
- Verification Result
- Policy Decision

---

## 14.12 Implementation Independence

This specification defines Proof behavior.

It does not prescribe:

- Measurement hardware
- Sensor technology
- Communication protocol
- Serialization format

Different implementations MAY produce different internal representations while preserving protocol compatibility.

---

## 14.13 Requirements Summary

Every compliant implementation SHALL satisfy the following requirements.

- Every Proof MUST be digitally signed.
- Every Proof MUST contain a unique nonce.
- Every Proof MUST be independently verifiable.
- Every Proof MUST be evaluated by the Policy Engine after successful verification.
- Successful Proof generation MUST NOT automatically result in token issuance.
